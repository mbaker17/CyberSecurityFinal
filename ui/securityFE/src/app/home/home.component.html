Common Terminology

Term Definition
1 Attack An action taken against a system in an attempt to gain access and information.
2 Back Door Hidden entry in software that bypassess security measures.
3 Brute Force Attack Simple computationally intense attack to gain access to a system/website
4 Encryption Transforming text into cipher text to keep the content a secrete.
5 Exploit To take advantage of a bug or vulnerability to compromise security.
6 Firewall Filter that prevents hackers from entering a system/software.
7 Keystroke logging The process of tracking keys that a target presses.
8 Phishing An email fraud method in an attempt to gather personal and financial information.
9 Spear Phishing Targeted Phishing.
10 Social Engineering The act of deceiving a target with the intent to aquire personal and financial information.
11 SQL Injection Code injection technique where SQL statemenets are entered into an entry field.
12 Virus Malicious program which is capable of copying itself and corrupting/destroying existing data.
13 Vulerability Weakness in which a hacker can comnpromise a system and its security.
14 Local Attack An attack on a system where the hacker had previous access to it.
15 Remote Attack An attack on a system where the hacker did not have previous access to it.
16 Trojan Horse A malicious program designed to look like a legit program.
17 Zero Day Exploit An undocumented or never before seen exploit.
18 Audit A system security evaluation.
19 Decryption Transforming cipher text to text.
20 Data Driven Attack An attack that is encoded in non suspiscous looking data which is executed by users.
21 Penetration Testing Threat modeling, a method of testing that targets individual components of an application.
21 Side-channel Attack Attack based on information gained from the implementation of a computer system.
